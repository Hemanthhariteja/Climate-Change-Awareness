Explore how rising CO2 levels have contributed to global warming, sea level rise, and more extreme weather events.

Explore the Data

Use the slider to explore data trends from 1980 to 2020

CO2 Levels Over Time: The chart shows a steady increase in CO2 concentrations from 338 ppm in 1980 to 415 ppm in 2020. This rise is a major driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial activities.

Global Temperature Anomalies: As CO2 levels increase, there is a corresponding rise in global temperature anomalies. The data indicates a rise from 0.2°C above the baseline in 1980 to 1.2°C in 2020. This warming trend impacts ecosystems, weather patterns, and sea ice.

Sea Level Rise: The increase in global temperatures accelerates the melting of glaciers and ice sheets, leading to rising sea levels. Our chart shows a rise of 100 mm over this period, contributing to coastal erosion, flooding, and habitat loss.

Frequency of Tropical Storms: Climate change is linked to more intense and frequent tropical storms. The data suggests an increase in the number of tropical storms, posing risks to coastal communities and infrastructure.
